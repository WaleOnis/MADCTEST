    SERVICES


    MADC Foundation (hereinafter referred to as "NGO Service Provider"), is a non profit organization domiciled in Lagos, Nigeria, with its principal place of business at
    13, Kadiri Street, Off Clegg Street, Ojuelegba, Surulere.

    The NGO Service Provider provides, among other programs:
    Health prevention and education programs on STD's, especially HIV prevention
    Education and Testing
    Community development aids
    Widows and single parental care assistants
    Old people and motherless baby care assistants
    Education and scolarships for the needy
    Provides business information and collaboration opportunities with Michigan industries and African Counterparts especially on ICT Mining, Agriculture, tourism, culture and other manufacturing sectors.

    The NGO Service Provider has the necessary skills, expertise and resources to provide such services.

    The Services will be performed in Nigeria and adjacent countries in Africa, which are the areas of expertise of NGO Services.


    01

    Agriculture

    Africa's varied agricultural exports play a significant role in global markets. Michigan's farming products and equipment provide perfect synergy for African business owners, boosting this essential part of the continent's economy.

    02

    Oil & Gas

    Africa, with its abundant oil and gas reserves, is a key exporter of these essential energy resources. This sector offers significant opportunities for businesses in Michigan.

    03

    Minerals & Metals

    Africa is a major supplier of diverse rare minerals and metals, making it a crucial provider for the automotive industry and other rapidly growing sectors globally.

    04

    Manufacturing

    Africa is rapidly developing its manufacturing base to service its young population and ecoming the new growth manufacturer region in the world. The expertise and manufacturing might of Michigan business is welcomed.

    05

    Education

    The growing youth population in Africa seeks greater access to educational opportunities. From K-12 to higher education, there are numerous prospects for Michigan and African organizations to collaborate, offering new hope and fostering stronger connections.

    06

    Human Capital

    The increasing population in Africa necessitates more resources and opportunities. Strengthening the relationship and mutual understanding between Michigan's businesses, educational institutions, and socially conscious organizations with their African counterparts will be beneficial for all parties involved.

    About Us


    Michigan African Development Council
    M.A.D.C

    The Michigan Africa Development Council is a Non-for-Profit Organization 501(c)(3) based in Michigan. Its goal is to foment business ties between private and public companies in Michigan and throughout African countries with emphasis in West Africa. Michigan companies, and subsequently the economy of the state will benefit from exposure to underserved African opportunities in emergent countries. The Michigan Africa Development Council (MADC) promotes Michigan businesses and opportunities in Africa.



    Africa is abundant with a diverse array of valuable resources.
